# Capital Raise Failure Diagnostic

## 1. Product Overview

**Name:** Capital Raise Failure Diagnostic  
**Type:** 2-5 minute pre-raise / active-raise diagnostic tool  
**Primary Users:** Emerging GPs, syndicators, fund managers, co-sponsors  
**Internal Users:** AEs, TAMs, Customer Success, Partnerships  
**Goal:** Identify where a GP's capital raise is most likely to break before it costs them investor momentum or funded capital.

## Core Value Proposition

Most capital raises do not fail because the GP lacks a better pitch deck. They fail because investor trust breaks at specific moments:

- Investor interest does not convert into signed subscriptions
- Follow-up becomes inconsistent
- The deal story does not survive diligence
- Signed investors delay funding
- Post-close operations look unprepared

The Capital Raise Failure Diagnostic helps GPs quickly identify their highest-risk failure point and understand what operational infrastructure is required to fix it.

## Primary Outcome

Answer the question:

> "Where is my raise most likely to lose capital, and what should I fix first?"

---

## 2. Strategic Purpose

This is not just a lead magnet.

It is a diagnostic wedge that should:

1. Deliver immediate value in under 5 minutes
2. Expose a painful, specific risk in the GP's raise
3. Route the buyer naturally toward the relevant CFP product
4. Qualify serious buyers based on raise size, timeline, current tooling, and active pain
5. Position CFP as the operating system for capital formation, not just an investor portal

---

## 3. Target Users

## Primary Users

### Emerging GPs

- Raising $2M-$50M
- Often using spreadsheets, email, DocuSign, Dropbox, or a basic portal
- Need help professionalizing the raise

### Repeat Syndicators

- Have raised before but still deal with manual follow-up, subscriptions, banking, and reporting
- Likely comparing CFP to InvestNext, Agora, Juniper Square, SyndicationPro, or AppFolio

### First-Time Fund Managers

- Need to understand what will break before they launch
- Often underestimate investor onboarding, admin, reporting, and LP diligence expectations

## Best-Fit Buyer Signals

Prioritize users who indicate:

- Active raise or launch within 90 days
- Raise size above $2M
- 25+ existing or target LPs
- Manual investor onboarding
- Current dissatisfaction with existing portal or spreadsheets
- Need for CRM, portal, fund admin, or migration support

## 5. Scoring Model

The diagnostic should produce one headline result plus five failure-mode sub-scores.

## Headline Output

**Capital Raise Failure Risk Score: 0-100**

Suggested interpretation:

- **0-30:** Low risk
- **31-60:** Moderate risk
- **61-80:** High risk
- **81-100:** Critical risk

Important: higher score = higher failure risk. This is more emotionally effective than a readiness score because it creates urgency.

---

## 6. Five Failure-Mode Sub-Scores

## 1. LP Onboarding Friction Risk

**Maps to:** Investor Portal  
**Failure mode:** Interested LPs stall before completing subscription.

Signals:

- Manual email/PDF process
- Too many investment steps
- No central portal
- Unclear doc access
- Manual KYC/accreditation
- No progress tracking

Example output:

> "Your highest risk is LP onboarding friction. Investors may express interest but stall before completing subscription because your process requires too many manual steps."

---

## 2. Pipeline Momentum Risk

**Maps to:** CRM  
**Failure mode:** Warm LPs go cold due to poor follow-up and weak pipeline visibility.

Signals:

- Spreadsheet/inbox-based tracking
- No defined stages
- Low confidence in capital forecast
- No automated follow-up
- No segmentation by LP status or likelihood

Example output:

> "Your raise is vulnerable to momentum decay. You may have investor interest, but without a structured pipeline, follow-ups and capital forecasting become unreliable."

---

## 3. LP Diligence Confidence Risk

**Maps to:** Underwriting  
**Failure mode:** LP trust weakens because the return story, assumptions, or downside case are not defensible.

Signals:

- Unclear return narrative
- Weak downside explanation
- Misalignment between deck/model/docs
- Unclear distribution logic
- No sensitivity analysis

Example output:

> "Your diligence confidence risk is elevated. LPs may hesitate if your return story, assumptions, and downside case are not presented clearly and consistently."

---

## 4. Funding Completion Risk

**Maps to:** Banking  
**Failure mode:** Signed investors delay or fail to fund because capital movement is disconnected from the subscription process.

Signals:

- Manual wire instructions
- Bank-record-based reconciliation
- No real-time funded status
- No integrated contribution tracking
- Unclear LP confirmation after funding

Example output:

> "Your funding completion risk is high. Signed commitments may not translate into received capital quickly if funding status and contribution tracking are handled manually."

---

## 5. Post-Close Credibility Risk

**Maps to:** Fund Administration  
**Failure mode:** The GP closes capital before having the operational infrastructure to manage investor reporting, distributions, tax workflows, and capital accounts.

Signals:

- No clear fund admin plan
- Manual investor ledgers
- Unclear K-1 responsibility
- Unclear distribution calculation workflow
- No reporting cadence
- No capital account process

Example output:

> "Your post-close credibility risk is elevated. LPs may question whether your back-office operations are ready to support investor reporting, distributions, tax workflows, and capital account accuracy."

---

## 7. Output Report

The output is the product. It cannot be generic.

## Required Output Components

### 1. Headline Diagnosis

Example:

> **Your Capital Raise Failure Risk: High**
>
> Your raise is most likely to lose momentum between **soft commit and funded capital** due to investor onboarding friction, weak pipeline control, and manual funding tracking.

---

### 2. Primary Failure Mode

Identify the single biggest risk.

Example:

> **Primary risk: LP onboarding friction**
>
> Your current process requires investors to move across multiple systems to review materials, sign documents, complete verification, and fund. This increases the risk that interested LPs delay or drop off before becoming funded investors.

---

### 3. Five Sub-Scores

Show scores visually:

- LP Onboarding Friction Risk: 78/100
- Pipeline Momentum Risk: 64/100
- LP Diligence Confidence Risk: 51/100
- Funding Completion Risk: 72/100
- Post-Close Credibility Risk: 44/100

---

### 4. Top 3 Capital Killers

Examples:

1. **Manual investor onboarding**
   - LPs may lose momentum when deal materials, subscription docs, verification, and funding instructions are disconnected.

2. **No reliable funded-capital forecast**
   - You may be counting soft interest as capital without enough visibility into stage, likelihood, or next step.

3. **Funding status is manually reconciled**
   - Signed investors may create false confidence if received funds are not tracked in the same workflow as commitments.

---

### 5. Recommended Fixes

Recommendations should map to product categories without being a hard sales pitch.

Example:

1. **Create a single investor flow from deal review to funding**
   - Investors should be able to review materials, sign, verify, and fund from one structured workflow.

2. **Run your raise through a defined LP pipeline**
   - Every investor should have a stage, likelihood, next step, and follow-up owner.

3. **Connect subscription status to received capital**
   - Avoid treating signed documents and funded commitments as separate workflows.

---

### 6. CFP Product Bridge

This should be subtle but obvious.

Example:

> GPs with lower failure risk typically have five infrastructure layers in place: a structured investor portal, a raise CRM, defensible underwriting workflows, integrated capital movement, and post-close fund administration support.

Then CTA:

> Want to see how this would look for your raise?

## 9. MVP Scope

Do **not** overbuild this.

## MVP Should Include

- Landing page
- 10-12 question diagnostic
- Rule-based scoring engine
- Five failure-mode sub-scores
- One primary risk diagnosis
- Top 3 capital killers
- 3 recommended fixes
- CTA based on highest-risk category
- Lead capture with qualification fields
- Internal notification to AE

## MVP Should Not Include

- File uploads
- AI deck analysis
- CRM integrations
- Bank integrations
- Full PDF report
- Dynamic benchmarking
- Login/account creation

Those can come later. If PM tries to add all that now, the scope is already rotting.

## 16. Risks and Guardrails

## Risk 1: Tool feels generic

Mitigation:

- Use blunt language
- Diagnose specific failure modes
- Avoid vague “best practices”

## Risk 2: Too many questions

Mitigation:

- Max 12 questions
- Use multiple-choice wherever possible
- Keep time under 5 minutes

## Risk 3: Weak product bridge

Mitigation:

- Every failure mode maps directly to a CFP product
- CTA changes based on primary risk

## Risk 4: Low-quality leads

Mitigation:

- Require raise size, timeline, and current system before full report
- Prioritize only high-intent leads internally

## Risk 5: Overpromising precision

Mitigation:

- Position as directional diagnostic, not guaranteed prediction
- Use language like “risk,” “likely,” and “may indicate”
